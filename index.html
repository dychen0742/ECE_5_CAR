What does PID stand for? What is the purpose of the P, the I, and the D? What steps did you take to choose the best values for your robot? Explain this like you would for someone taking this course next year.

  PID stands for Proportional integral derivative. The purpose of P is to be a scalar multiple that would help to transfer our error into the desired turn rate. The purpose of I which is the integral is to sum the previous errors like memory and will help elimnate small and steady state errors. Increasing kI on the potentiometer is proportional to the sumerror and will help decrease the error that the proportional control was not able to get rid of. The purpose of D which is the derivative is to predict increases and decreases in error in preparation for turns, essentially determining how it will perform at sharp turns. If the rate of change is very high like at sharp corners, it will correct the turn accordingly.
